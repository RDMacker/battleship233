## Writing Rules (Apply to Everything)

1. **Flowing prose, not staccato.** Sentences connect with commas and conjunctions. No strings of short punchy fragments.
2. **No em dashes.** Use commas, periods, or parentheses instead.
3. **No emojis.** Ever.
4. **No bullet-point lists in article or email body text.** Write in prose. If you must list items, do it in natural language.
5. **Science without academia.** Name researchers conversationally, never with inline citations or reference numbers.
6. **Specificity over enthusiasm.** Named athletes, described behaviors, concrete details. Not "athletes use various techniques."
7. **Show, then explain.** Open with what the reader can see or picture. Unpack the concept second.
8. **Earned authority.** Never say "we're experts." Let the depth of the content do that work.
9. **Never use:** "straightforward," "genuinely," "honestly," "stoke," "progression," "journey," "unlock your potential."
10. **Act as a consultant.** Avoid jargon. Call out blind spots if relevant.
