# Content Think Tank: pubgolf — 2026-07-14

Here's the state of the round. The World Cup final lands Sunday July 19, and it's turned personalised football shirts into the stag uniform of the summer. The whole country is already in fancy dress. They just need a course to play. Meanwhile the UK's biggest stag-and-hen operator, StagWeb's parent Groupia, has gone into administration and stopped taking bookings, so there's a planning vacuum and a live pool of best men with no plan and four days' notice. The hens are out-spending you and out-planning you. Max spend up 59% since 2023, booked nine months out, while your crew plans in the taxi. On Instagram the only number that matters now is the send. A share to the group chat weighs three-to-five times a like, and the platform is actively demoting AI-polished content in favour of raw phone footage. That is the shareable scorecard's home turf. And the stag conversation has moved on from the old cliches. It's about money, admin and who's actually in the game now. Every idea below plays to that.

## The slate

### Ship this stag season

These are flat. Green or gold background, the flag pin as the one mark, fictional example numbers, no photos of a real bar or a real drink. No build, no user footage needed. You can make all of them this week.

**Last place is a carousel now.**
*Instagram carousel · Boldness 1/5*
- **Concept.** The scorecard is the product, so let the recap do the marketing. One slide per hole (the par, the drink, the standout stroke or penalty), building to a final slide that names the wooden spoon and hands over the green jacket. Ships now as a fill-in-the-blanks template the host drops the crew's real numbers into the morning after.
- **Hook.** Cover slide: "Eighteen holes. One wooden spoon. Swipe to find out who."
- **Why now.** Carousels are Instagram's top-engagement format in 2026 (0.52% vs Reels' 0.50%, socialinsider's 15M-post study), and the swipe-to-the-loser reveal is built to be fired into the group chat.
- **Critics flagged.** The most generic format on the slate, but it is the core asset, so keep the template dead simple or the hungover host never fills it in. Cut two versions off the one card: a sub-15s single-hole teaser for strangers, the full round for the lads. *(Merge: absorbs producer-3; the "real phone photos" texture becomes flat design.)*

**Your back nine for Sunday.**
*Instagram carousel · Boldness 2/5*
- **Concept.** A ready-to-run course card that maps the crawl onto final weekend: holes named Group Stage, Golden Boot, Extra Time, Penalties, each with a par and a forfeit. The template drops in the groom's surname so it reads like the back of his shirt.
- **Hook.** Cover slide: "England on tour Sunday. Here's your course card for the final."
- **Why now.** The final lands Sunday July 19 (MetLife, 8pm BST), and personalised football-shirt fancy dress is the summer's dominant stag uniform because it survives any door policy. The trend peaks this exact week.
- **Critics flagged.** Strongest of the World Cup cluster, but it's dead the second the whistle blows, so ship by July 18 or kill it. Keep the shirt gag clear of real club and kit trademarks. *(Merge: absorbs thought-leader-6; the hens hook plays as its own post below.)*

**The best man's an unpaid events agency. Promote him.**
*Instagram Reel · Boldness 3/5*
- **Concept.** A taggable Reel aimed straight at the buyer. Open on the truest, funniest line on the slate, cut through the job he's drowning in (twelve WhatsApps a day, chasing payments, sorting passports), then cast the group chat (the fun sponge, the fun prevention officer, the debt collector) and promote the debt collector from unpaid accountant to Course Setter, the one who builds the holes and rules the card.
- **Hook.** First three seconds, deadpan to camera: "You're not the best man. You're an unpaid events agency."
- **Why now.** Every 2026 stag guide warns best men off the one-man-events-agency burnout, and the group chat already roasts itself with these exact names. The app takes scoring and headcount off his plate so he can finally play hole 9 instead of refereeing it.
- **Critics flagged.** Keep it as the tag-bait engagement play and drop any "real spend per pint" claim the app can't back yet. *(Merge: collapses the whole best-man cluster into one, insider-2's line plus insider-3's tag format; thought-leader-2 folds in.)*

**Your designated driver just won the crawl.**
*Instagram Reel · Boldness 4/5*
- **Concept.** The category assumes the sober one is a lift home and an asterisk. Put him on top of the card instead. Non-drinkers play at par 1, the sober Caddy is a named official whose scoring call is final, and a player alternating pints and waters can win the round outright. The recap builds to the winner reveal, and it's the mate everyone wrote off as the driver. Same scorecard, no asterisk, full bragging rights.
- **Hook.** First three seconds: "Most stags think the sober one holds the coats. He's holding the pen. And the green jacket."
- **Why now.** Zebra striping is a mainstream 2026 drinking pattern, and pub golf's own rulebook already hands the sober Caddy the final say. The proof point is real, not aspirational.
- **Critics flagged.** One of the best on the slate and fully shippable in flat design. Keep it playful so it never tips into worthy or a lecture.

**Pick a hole. Live with what you get.**
*Instagram carousel · Boldness 3/5*
- **Concept.** A Follow-the-Line carousel where the flag pin, our one brand mark, snakes across the slides. The swiper picks a number on the cover and follows their line to the drink and forfeit they've been dealt. Rules explainer and pre-crawl hype in one, and it reskins per season (the World Cup edition drops straight in). A par-1 line is dealt too, so the Caddy and the zebra-striper get a real hole, never an asterisk.
- **Hook.** Cover slide: "Pick your hole, 1 to 18. Follow the pin. No takebacks."
- **Why now.** Follow-the-Line reveals are big enough that Instagram's own @creators account posts them, and "I got hole 7, the Guinness one, send help" is a screenshot the crew has to pass on.
- **Critics flagged.** The ownable one, because the pin-line and reskinnable skeleton make a borrowed format ours, so make the pin-line central. *(Merge: survivor of the pick-a-hole cluster, kills analyst-4 in and folds its par-1 slide here.)*

**A spilled pint isn't bad luck. It's two strokes.**
*Instagram Reel series · Boldness 4/5*
- **Concept.** One writing template that covers the whole rulebook: deadpan contrarian claim in the first three seconds, hard cut to the rule that proves it. The flagship episode is the water hazard: a spilled drink is a two-stroke penalty, and the spill plays out in flat graphics, not a real pint. Then run the series (the bunker, the mulligan, lying about strokes is a five-stroke penalty, the Caddy's call is final).
- **Hook.** First three seconds: "Most stags think a spilled pint's just bad luck." Beat. "Two-stroke penalty."
- **Why now.** 2026's top-performing hooks are contrarian openers landing in the first 1.7-3 seconds, and Instagram is demoting AI-polish in favour of raw. Someone forwards it to the mate who always spills, captioned "this is you."
- **Critics flagged.** The contrarian open is the most saturated hook of the year, so execution has to differentiate, and the spill must be flat design (the no-real-drinks rule), not footage that doesn't exist. *(Merge: producer-6 is the series spine, thought-leader-5 the flagship episode, analyst-3 folded in.)*

**Hens plan nine months out. You plan in the taxi.**
*Instagram Reel · Boldness 2/5*
- **Concept.** Own the stereotype and flip it. Split-screen the hen spreadsheet against the silent stag WhatsApp, then the crew builds a full course in the time it takes to order the first round, and the finished scorecard is the proof the taxi-planners still pulled it off.
- **Hook.** First three seconds: "Hens booked this nine months ago. You've got till the taxi arrives."
- **Why now.** Hen max spend is up 59% since 2023 versus 15% for stags, and hens are nearly 3x more likely to book 9-12 months ahead. A stat with built-in stag-vs-hen tension and a gap the app closes in five minutes.
- **Critics flagged.** True, funny, instantly taggable, but let the scorecard payoff carry it, and only claim what the app does. Say "builds a course in five minutes," and skip the "AI planner" line unless that feature has actually shipped.

**No operator. No problem.**
*Instagram Reel · Boldness 2/5*
- **Concept.** Aimed at the best men staring down an operator-less weekend. No tour company, no nine-month lead time, no download, no paywall. A host opens the free web app, builds a course, the crew tees off tonight. Screen-rec plus flat text, positioned against both the collapsed operator and the paywalled app-store rivals.
- **Hook.** First three seconds, deadpan to camera: "The company that plans stag dos just shut its doors. Your round's still on."
- **Why now.** Groupia (StagWeb, GoHen) entered administration and stopped taking bookings, and rivals are already publishing "StagWeb alternative" guides. There's a pool of groups hunting for a plan right now.
- **Critics flagged.** Timely capture window, but don't dance on job losses. Drop the "went bust, good" gloat, lead with "no operator, no problem," and close on an actual course so it isn't just a free-app ad.

### Needs a build first (roadmap, not a July post)

These are strong. They just aren't July posts, because the real version needs a product build. Ship the flat bridge now, put the feature on the roadmap, and the content writes itself once it's live.

**Somebody has to be Player of the Crawl.**
*Scorecard feature (build), flat award-card template ships now · Boldness 4/5*
- **Concept.** The card grows a second headline award beside the wooden spoon. A hero to crown, not just a loser to roast, tied to one brutally simple number nobody can argue with. The Caddy can win it, because best scorer doesn't have to be the biggest drinker, so par-1 players are in the running. Hero and villain on one card, built to be sent.
- **Hook.** The card flips to a gold panel: "Player of the Crawl. One name. One number."
- **Why now.** The research says pair a positive superlative with the wooden spoon (Sidemen's Man of the Match) and tie every award to one simple number, the way the Golden Boot travels and the "best vibes" award never does.
- **Critics flagged.** Cheap, high-leverage, and it upgrades the lead asset, but the auto-naming is a scorecard build. **Bridge:** ship a flat "Player of the Crawl" award-card template now; auto-generation is the roadmap piece.

**Your stag town, ranked by value par.**
*Web leaderboard page (build), flat carousel ships now · Boldness 3/5*
- **Concept.** A "value par" league table ranking UK stag towns by pints per pound, so Newcastle's £5.15 average laps London's £6.87. Crews screenshot it to justify where they're taking the groom, and the comments fight their own town up the board. It travels well beyond the current audience.
- **Hook.** Leaderboard reveal: "London stags pay £6.87 a pint. Newcastle stags pay £5.15 and win the card."
- **Why now.** The Stag Company crowned Newcastle "stag capital" on a pure value argument. This turns cost-of-living griping into rankable bragging rights.
- **Critics flagged.** Fresh and share-baiting, but the live table is a page-and-data build. **Bridge:** ship a flat town-ranking carousel now off public pint-price data. "Value par" bends real golf vocab, so use it knowingly.

**The best man's not overcharging. Here's the card.**
*Instagram carousel · Boldness 3/5*
- **Concept.** Answers the recurring "where did my £200 go" row. Open on the accusation every best man has seen, then swipe-reveal the card that settles it: par per hole, every round accounted for, argument dead before it starts.
- **Hook.** Cover slide: "Is the best man overcharging us for the stag? (a thread)"
- **Why now.** "Best man overcharging" is a recurring named genre on MoneySavingExpert and Mumsnet. A card that shows the receipts settles it.
- **Critics flagged.** Hits a real nerve, but a stroke scorecard isn't an expense-splitter. **Bridge:** ship the flat "here's the card" version that answers the row with holes, pars and strokes; the itemised money receipt needs spend-tracking built first. Keep the cover reading native, not a manufactured fake thread.

**Throw the Hammer.**
*Product feature (build), flat house-rules card ships now · Boldness 5/5*
- **Concept.** One card, thrown once a crawl, that doubles what's at stake on a single hole, lifted wholesale from TGL (Tiger and Rory's indoor league). Reward and wooden-spoon risk both double on that hole only. The moment is the throw: the crew ambushing the groom on the hardest hole. Plays identically for the lad on par 1.
- **Hook.** A HAMMER card hits a sticky table: "Double or nothing. And we're aiming it at you."
- **Why now.** TGL's on-demand Hammer is the exact structure the research flags stealing for a once-per-round highlight moment.
- **Critics flagged.** Genuinely novel and the golf twist is load-bearing, but the in-app card is a build and the throw is real-bar footage. **Bridge:** ship a flat "house rules" card now (one Hammer per crawl, double the hole, tracked on the paper scorecard, zero engineering).

## Wildcards worth the risk

Bolder bets the critics split on. Real upside, real catch. Here's the catch, said plainly.

**Give the groom a walk-on.**
*UGC-seed Reel, flat "tale of the tape" stat-card ships now · Boldness 5/5*
- **Concept.** Film the crew's arrival at hole one like a darts walk-on. The groom gets a nickname, a walk-on song the group picked, and a "tale of the tape" stat card (age, years since his last proper session, pints-per-hour PB), with a mate on the mic screaming him through the door. Nobody in pub golf films the entrance, so it's broadcast-grade atmosphere, shot stone-cold sober before a drop is drunk, which is why it always lands.
- **Hook.** Black screen, one bar of walk-on music, then "on the oche... the groom" as the door swings open.
- **Why now.** Darts turned walk-on entrances into its most viral asset, and the research says give every group a walk-on song and film the entrance as its own clip.
- **The risk, plainly.** It needs real footage of a real venue arrival. The brand can't shoot that (no-real-bar rule) and there's no user footage yet. So this is the hero UGC format to seed, not an owned July post. Ship the flat "tale of the tape" stat-card template now as the thing that feeds it, and hand crews the prompt.

**There's a Bandit on the card.**
*Product game mode (build), flat house-rules card ships now · Boldness 5/5*
- **Concept.** A secret-saboteur side-bet run over a normal crawl, lifted from The Traitors. One player is quietly the Bandit (golf slang for a cheat), racking up penalty strokes on everyone else and tanking the groom without getting caught. At the last hole the group votes; guess wrong and the Bandit steals the round. Hens already run Traitors missions and stags don't.
- **Hook.** Whispered over a shaky table shot: "One of these six is cheating the card. And it's not who you think."
- **Why now.** The research names The Traitors the format of the decade, already in hen-do missions but not stags. This beats the inevitable Traitors-themed stag package to market.
- **The risk, plainly.** It needs a game-mode build, and a hidden saboteur is hard to run across a noisy, drunk crawl with no enforcement. **Bridge to test appetite:** a flat "house rules" side-bet card the group self-runs and votes on. The cheapest way to find out if the Traitors-for-stags itch is real before anyone builds the mode.

**What happens on tour is all on the scorecard.**
*Instagram Reel · Boldness 4/5*
- **Concept.** Hijack "what happens on tour stays on tour" and flip it for the one person who hates hearing it, the groom's partner. The gag: the scorecard is the receipts, and the most incriminating thing logged all night is that Dave four-putted hole 6 and took the wooden spoon. A public recap becomes reassurance instead of a buzzkill.
- **Hook.** Text on screen over a flat card reveal: "What happens on tour... stays on the scorecard."
- **Why now.** Grooms' partners are visibly anxious about stag-night secrecy, and nobody else works this angle.
- **The risk, plainly.** It's aimed at the groom's partner, a share-driver, not the core buyer, and "everything's logged" can read as undercutting the night. Keep it a supporting post, not a lead, and note the four-putt gag can also just live inside a normal recap.

## Killed, and why

The bin filled up with sameness and with ideas that quietly assumed footage the brand can't shoot. Three separate scorecard-reveal carousels and three near-identical "most stags get this wrong" hazard Reels collapsed into one of each, because the contrarian cold-open is the single most saturated hook of 2026 and the slate only needs one series spine. A whole tier died on the no-real-bar rule: the Wimbledon-style getting-ready montage, the Flash-filtered group photo as an owned post, the single-take 18-man crawl, the two-cut editing workflow. All of them need real lads in a real venue, which makes them UGC formats to seed later, not July assets the brand can make itself. Several crowd-pleasers turned out to be product features wearing a content costume (the Hammer, the Bandit mode, the auto-named Player of the Crawl, the itemised money receipt, the live value-par leaderboard) and got moved to the roadmap with a flat bridge each. And one got killed on principle. "Wrecked is a score, not a strategy" had a sharp line, but the pace-yourself thesis drifts toward moralizing about drinking, which is a brand no-go. The designated-driver piece makes the exact same players welcome without ever lecturing anyone, so that's the one that survives.
