# Content Think Tank: sportsense — 2026-07-15

On the channels sportsense actually runs (Instagram and Meta first, with Reels, carousels and Stories, then YouTube Shorts as a second discovery surface, and email plus the monthly newsletter as the owned layer), the reach signals have quietly shifted underneath everyone. Private sends (the DM forward) and saves have overtaken likes as what the ranking rewards, with sends now weighted roughly three to five times a like, and raw phone-shot face-to-camera has started to out-share polished production as AI floods the feed. Carousels remain the highest-engagement feed unit and get a second life through the slide-2 re-serve, where a follower who ignores slide one is re-shown the post 24 to 48 hours later with slide two as the cover, so both slides have to stand alone as hooks. Reels win cold reach only when the hook lands inside the first 1 to 3 seconds. Sitting over all of it is the argument this audience is actually having, human coaching versus a wave of automated apps, sharpened this quarter by a brand-new fully automated MTB coaching app that runs a rider end to end with no human in the loop, and by the finding that 91% of coaches already use AI while insisting it cannot replace the relationship. That tension, not a feature list, is the opening.

## The slate

One rule runs through every surviving format below. The sportsense overlay (the skeletal frame, the joint angles) stays in as evidence and never as the star, because the primary audience is skeptical, revenue-motivated coaches who read a product-forward clip as a software ad and scroll past it. Each format leads with the coach's problem, the rider, or the myth, and the overlay appears only as the proof.

### Reels and Shorts

**heels down is the most misunderstood cue on the trail**
Format: Short (cross-posts unchanged as a Reel). Boldness: 3/5.
Concept: The repeatable spine is one widely repeated MTB cue per installment, stated in the first second as if it were true, then disproved with a single rigorous overlay showing the mechanism, mechanism named, out before 45 seconds. It never runs out because MTB is full of half-true cues, so every post is the next one on the list, and installment one opens on a less-saturated cue than "get your weight back" (that exact video is over-made), framing "heels down" as misunderstood rather than wrong so experienced coaches engage instead of pushing back on a strawman.
Hook: "everyone says heels down, and the joint angles show what it actually does to your grip."
Why now: the descending-cue myths are still fought publicly (Pinkbike ZEP, DirtSmart), Shorts reward one idea with the keep-watching decision at or before the one-second mark, and myth-busting reliably triggers DM shares.
Critics flagged: keep the overlay as evidence not star, dial the absolutism back to "misunderstood cue," and make the overlay rigorous because myth-busting is exactly where an expert audience checks your work.
Merged from cluster (c), survivor producer-5's fixed spine, absorbing analyst-4, insider-2 and thoughtleader-4.

**four seconds of one line, and the coach calls it first**
Format: Reel. Boldness: 4/5.
Concept: The repeatable spine is four seconds of a real line, a freeze on the tell, the coach committing his own call (green, blue or black) on screen before the reveal, then one overlay frame isolating the single tell that gave it away. It never runs out because every submitted or rostered clip is the next episode, and a running public accuracy scoreboard gives people a reason to return and check the coach's record, while the comment field fills with exactly the clips the remote tier runs on, so it doubles as the submission funnel. The "trail grade a rider belongs on" gatekeeping phrasing is dropped.
Hook: "four seconds of this line, I am committing my call on screen before the overlay, comment yours (green, blue or black) before I freeze it."
Why now: guess-the-rank now has an official TikTok Guessing Game polling feature and no MTB coach runs the diagnostic-guess format systematically, so a trained read becomes a participatory Reel that feeds the funnel.
Critics flagged: overlay strictly as reveal-evidence so the coach's eye stays the star, and the commit-first accountability plus the scoreboard are the only non-stock parts, so keep them.
Merged from cluster (a), survivor producer-1, absorbing maverick-1's commit-on-screen accountability and accuracy scoreboard.

**the same rider, week one next to week eight, overlay on**
Format: Reel. Boldness: 1/5.
Concept: The repeatable spine is one athlete's same feature filmed at week one of remote coaching and again weeks later, placed side by side with the overlay so the body-position change is measured, not claimed, and it never runs out because every athlete milestone is the next post. The rider's transformation stays the star so the overlay reads as measurement rather than a software demo, and no week-one is ever staged, which means the first post is roughly eight weeks out and roster baselines have to be captured starting now.
Hook: "this rider cased the same jump every session for a month, so here is week one on the left and week eight on the right."
Why now: the single-clip before-and-after reveal is among the most reliable save-and-send MTB formats, proven by Fabio Wibmer's before-and-after at nearly 20M views and already run by working MTB skills coaches.
Critics flagged: highest-scored standalone on the slate but footage-gated, so start capturing baselines now and never fake a week-one (see build order).

**an app is not your coach, and it should not try to be**
Format: Reel. Boldness: 5/5.
Concept: The fear under every coach's skepticism is that software wants to replace the relationship, so rather than argue, Rich says it first, straight into a phone camera with no production, that an app is not your coach and it should not try to be. The repeatable spine is the honest concession followed by the line that separates the categories, a score tells a rider what happened while a coach tells them why and what to change, and it refreshes every time a new automated tool ships. It is de-named on purpose, speaking to the whole category of fully automated apps rather than calling out any product, which keeps it a pure trust play with zero legal or tone exposure.
Hook: "an app is not your coach, and it should not try to be."
Why now: a fully automated MTB coaching app launched mid-May 2026 with explicitly no human in the loop, 91% of coaches use AI while insisting it cannot replace connection, and raw face-to-camera is what the feed now rewards.
Critics flagged: drop the competitor naming and the "wants to delete you" alarmism, and make the raw delivery land as a person, not a tagline.
Merged from cluster (f), survivor insider-6, folding in thoughtleader-2's "what happened versus why and what to change" line de-named, absorbing analyst-6, thoughtleader-2 and thoughtleader-6.

**the four-grand bike and the zero-dollar rider**
Format: Reel. Boldness: 4/5.
Concept: Bike culture runs on the belief that the next frame or wheelset is the upgrade, so this Reel opens by calling it out plainly, that you spent four grand on the bike and nothing on the rider and everyone on the trail can see it, then makes the quiet case that structured coaching is the upgrade you skipped. The repeatable spine is the provocation-then-turn, engineered as an argument riders defend, attack and tag a mate on, and it never runs out because the gear-versus-skill fight is permanent, with sportsense present only as the soft landing and the tone calibrated to provoke rather than sneer so it does not alienate the gear-aspirational riders who share it.
Hook: "you spent four grand on the bike and nothing on the rider, and everyone on the trail can see it."
Why now: "it's the rider not the bike" splits polls near 50/50, and the belt-drive and gearbox news cycle (Gates' unclaimed €100k bounty into 2026) has the culture arguing about equipment again.
Critics flagged: sharpen the funnel to sportsense specifically and provoke, do not sneer.

Trial Reels are not a standalone post. They are the hook-testing method layered onto the hero Reels above (the one-myth Short, the guess-the-level Reel and the before-and-after), shoot one mechanism twice with two different openings, publish both to non-followers first, and promote only the version that earns sends, since sends are the signal that predicts reach and there is no grid downside at zero followers.

### Carousels

**same corner, four riders, one rung is yours**
Format: Carousel. Boldness: 4/5.
Concept: The repeatable spine is the same corner or skill climbed one grade per slide (green, blue, black, double black), each middle slide an annotated freeze with the joint angles labelled, the cover withholding the punchline so slide one makes no sense without a swipe and slide two written as its own standalone hook for the re-serve. It puts an actual number on the gap, the degrees of hip hinge between one rung and the next, and it never runs out because every skill and corner has a ladder. The copyrighted World Cup frame is dropped from the top rung, the ladder caps at levels filmable from the roster, and the labels stay about the rider's body, not a spec sheet for the tool.
Hook (cover): "same corner ridden four ways, and the only difference is measured in degrees, swipe to find your rung."
Why now: the beginner-versus-pro levels comparison is actively viral across trades, art and cooking, and carousels are the highest-engagement feed format (~1.92% versus ~0.50% for Reels) with a slide-2 re-serve.
Critics flagged: drop the World Cup top rung on rights, and keep the labels on the rider's body so it teaches rather than pitches.

**you are not losing athletes to price**
Format: Carousel. Boldness: 3/5.
Concept: Coaches keep blaming price for churn when the real leak is the silence between sessions, so this teardown is built on the re-serve, slide one and slide two each written as a standalone hook so both the first impression and the 24-to-48-hour re-serve land, three annotated freeze-frames in the middle each carrying one mechanism as quiet evidence, and a save-and-send close. The repeatable spine is one coach-economics number walked to the infrastructure fix, and it never runs out because there is a deep well of those numbers. It uses the exact sentence coaches trade so it reads like eavesdropping, and it is anchored to MTB-specific 2026 benchmarks ($150 to $400 a month, roughly 10% monthly churn, a renewal call four to six weeks before a package ends) rather than the borrowed physiotherapy retention stat.
Hook (slide one): "you are not losing athletes to price, you are losing them to the gap between sessions." Slide two cover: "you're reading 'i can't come more often' completely wrong."
Why now: carousels out-engage Reels with a re-serve that promotes slide two to cover, a second impression most accounts waste, and the 2026 MTB coaching benchmarks make the math concrete.
Critics flagged: strongest of the price-gap cluster, but its save-and-send propagation needs a seed coach audience, so it is not a t-zero starter (see build order).
Merged from cluster (b), survivor producer-2's two-cover mechanic carrying insider-1's exact-words voice and thoughtleader-1's "reading it wrong" reframe, absorbing analyst-1, insider-1 and thoughtleader-1.

### Stories

**pause here, where is this rider losing speed** (deferred)
Format: Story. Boldness: 3/5.
Concept: The repeatable spine is a fixed weekly loop, a poll over a paused rider frame, the reveal with a single overlay as evidence, then a question sticker asking coaches the line their athletes give when they cancel, with every reply answered by DM. It never runs out because any clip has a decisive frame and the mechanic never changes, and it rehearses the two-way remote loop in public while gathering the exact language sportsense should speak back. It is deferred, not killed, because Stories have no cold discovery and reach only an existing following.
Hook: "pause here, is this rider losing speed at entry or exit, tap it before you swipe."
Why now: the 2026 Stories update weights replies, sticker taps and the DMs they open, the poll sticker earns the most taps for the least effort, and pause-and-guess drives roughly 50% more engagement.
Critics flagged: all three critics flag no cold discovery at zero audience, so defer until the Reels and Shorts funnel has built a follower base, and let clip and overlay quality carry it rather than the generic poll-question-DM mechanic.
Merged from cluster (d), survivor producer-3, folding in maverick-3's pause-and-guess "commit before the reveal" hook, absorbing analyst-3, insider-4 and maverick-3.

### Email and newsletter

**the athlete who cannot come more often**
Format: Email (welcome). Boldness: 1/5.
Concept: The one line every coach hears opens a welcome email that fires within minutes of a lead signing up, naming that sentence on the first screen as a recurring-revenue gap rather than a sales objection, 150 to 300 words, a single CTA repeated once. The repeatable spine is the template itself, every new lead hits it at peak intent and it reuses for re-engagement with one value point swapped, so it never runs dry. It supports the funnel rather than building audience, near-zero ongoing burden, no competitor or rights risk.
Hook (subject): "the athlete who cannot come more often."
Why now: welcome and lead emails convert best within roughly five minutes of signup at 150 to 300 words with a single first-screen CTA.
Critics flagged: table-stakes owned-layer plumbing that does its job, coordinate the shared "cannot come more often" sentence with the gap carousel.

**the math before the mention**
Format: Newsletter (template). Boldness: 2/5.
Concept: The owned container is a fixed monthly skeleton, an italicized TL;DR, a coach-economics number before sportsense is named, exactly two workflows each with a short capitalized action label and a benefit subhead, and a single "reply to this email" ask, engineered to be forwarded. The repeatable spine is the template, and it never runs out because each edition swaps in a new stat-led open and a different real athlete, so the fee argument and the 91% AI angle both run as editions rather than one-off newsletters. The stat-led open leans on the MTB-specific 2026 benchmarks, and sportsense appears only as the infrastructure that makes the math work.
Hook (subject, illustrative): "your renewal call is happening four weeks too late."
Why now: creator newsletters run 35 to 50% opens on six-to-ten-word benefit subject lines, and two focused workflows outperform three.
Critics flagged: a spine, not a hero, so build it now as the container the fee and AI editions plug into and let the Reels and Shorts funnel fill the list.

**the $250 you pay on every $5,000**
Format: Newsletter (edition of the template). Boldness: 3/5.
Concept: Coaches rarely add up what their software takes until someone does the math, so this edition opens on the number before it names anything, a 5% transaction fee is $250 skimmed off every $5,000 a coach earns, then lands the reframe that a tool built to skim a percentage of your revenue is the wrong category, while the right infrastructure earns revenue by hosting a paid remote tier. The repeatable spine is the template, this is its fee installment, and the model argument refreshes as pricing keeps moving. Every named-competitor quote and every one-star review is stripped, the argument stays on the model and the economics, because competitor-mocking undercuts the trust play before any value is established.
Hook (subject): "the $250 hidden in every $5,000 you earn."
Why now: 2026 competitor pricing moves have put cost in coaches' feeds, and the fee math is sharp on its own without naming anyone.
Critics flagged: strip every named-competitor quote (unanimous legal and tone risk) and argue the model, not the rival.
Merged from cluster (e), the de-named fee argument as an edition of the newsletter template, absorbing insider-5 and thoughtleader-5, with insider-3's read-the-reviews-aloud version killed.

## Wildcards worth the risk

**one frame off the podium**
Format: Short. Boldness: 5/5.
After each round the coach pulls one frame from the winning descent, stands in front of it with the overlay behind him, points at the single trait that separated first from third, then says the same overlay reads a Tuesday-night rider the same way, which bridges the pro to the amateur and rides a race story people already watch (Williams back-to-back, Höll four of five). The risk is plain and hard, current World Cup broadcast footage is copyrighted, cannot be cheaply licensed, and draws copyright strikes on a commercial account, so this is viable only if broadcast-footage rights are actually cleared and is otherwise killed. It is also perishable, so if rights clear it ships within days of a round.

**score this line before you scroll**
Format: Newsletter. Boldness: 4/5.
The rate-this-1-10 device becomes a monthly diagnostic the reader answers before scrolling, commit your score out of ten, then the coach gives his number and the two mechanism reasons below the fold and asks the reader to reply with a clip of their own athlete for next month, turning the most one-way channel into a two-way submission loop. The risk is threefold, it needs a subscriber list that does not exist at zero state, it requires explicit consent before publishing a subscriber's athlete, and it is a third guess-and-reveal instance, so it must be the one recurring interactive issue and spaced from the guess-the-level Reel and the Stories loop.

**the comment gets a video back**
Format: Reel. Boldness: 4/5.
This is the boldest strategic bet on the slate for demonstrating two-way coaching, the coach takes a real riding question, sits the comment on screen for a beat, then answers with a 20-second overlay demo and one cue, performing on camera the exact responsiveness this audience is most afraid of losing. The risk is supply, inbound questions do not exist at zero state, so seed it from the real questions the coaching inbox already gets ("a question I get") until the audience writes the brief, and keep any one-way-app jab off-screen so it demonstrates the differentiator instead of taking a shot.

## Killed, and why

The kill list is mostly a story about convergence. Five personas generating six ideas each landed on the same six formats, so most of the thirty entries were duplicate instances of one another (the gap-not-price carousel appeared four times, the one-myth Short and the AI-versus-human position four times each, the Stories loop four times), and the merges above kept the single strongest version of each and retired the rest rather than shipping the same idea five ways. A second pattern was an "overlay-as-star" smell running through several drafts, formats that led with the sportsense skeletal overlay as the hero, which reads as software marketing to an audience of skeptical, revenue-motivated coaches, so every survivor was rebuilt to lead with the coach's problem, the rider or the myth and to hold the overlay back as evidence. The third pattern carried real tone and legal exposure, a cluster of ideas that named competitors on screen, read their one-star reviews aloud, or pinned a specific injury on a named brand. Reading a rival's reviews aloud was killed outright as the clearest hit-piece on the slate, the "adaptive plan caused an injury" Story was killed as written because it names a brand and attributes an injury (defamation exposure) and because Stories have no cold reach anyway, and the fee argument and the AI-versus-human position were both kept but fully de-named, arguing the model and the category instead of the rival.

## The build order

Because sportsense has little existing audience and almost no footage library yet, the sequence matters more than a flat ranking, and the footage dependency decides what ships first.

1. Start with the one-myth-one-overlay Short. It is the single best zero-state start, the only format whose footage dependency is fully met on day one (Rich self-staging one cue with his own overlay), the least ad-smell because it is pure teaching, and it cross-posts to Shorts unchanged for a second discovery surface.
2. Run the guess-the-rider's-level Reel next as the funnel. It travels on cold reach, guessing drives comments and tagging drives sends, and its comment field generates the clip submissions the before-and-after Reel, the carousels and the Stories loop all depend on.
3. Add "an app is not your coach" and the "four-grand bike, zero-dollar rider" Reels for cheap cold reach. Both are just Rich to a phone camera with no footage supply and no competitor or rights risk, and they carry the trust position and the argument that travel farthest cold.
4. Start capturing roster baselines now so the before-and-after Reel can ship in roughly eight weeks. It is the highest-ceiling proof format on the slate but gated on real week-one footage, so the clock starts today and no week-one is ever staged.
5. Stand up the newsletter template as the owned container. Build the skeleton now and let the Reels and Shorts funnel fill the list, so the fee edition and the 91% AI edition have somewhere to live.
6. Defer the Stories loop and the carousel propagation until a follower base exists. Stories have no cold discovery and the carousel save-and-send engine needs a seed coach audience, so both wait on the reach the Reels and Shorts build first.
