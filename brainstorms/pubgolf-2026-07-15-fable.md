**The Hazard Index** — flat-asset series, the cleared paid layer. Every meme character that proves itself organically becomes a numbered flat hazard card — hazard 7: the bunker, his round, twenty-five minutes in the toilets and counting — compiling quarterly into a save-and-laminate index for the pre-crawl chat. Starts August, no fixed cadence, only proven characters carded, winning caption copy kept verbatim. The risk is sequencing: it is a paid system built on performance data that does not exist yet, and if cards get made before the memes ratify the characters, the whole layer is guesswork wearing a scorecard.

**The Fourball** — collab program, matchplay-framed co-authored posts. Fixtures announced like matchplay ties, the template chosen per partner rather than the same handshake every fortnight, working a target list from golf meme pages down to local crawl accounts. Run it target-list-first: build the list now, ship the first fixture only when a partner has signed, and do not call it fortnightly until two are banked. The risk is the dependency: at near-zero followers there is nothing to trade, every approach is a cold call, and the timeline is not ours to set — the reach math only pays if someone answers.

## Killed, and why

The kills clustered into patterns worth remembering. Five personas independently pitched the same Caddy tribunal — proof the idea is the brand's centre of gravity, and proof the room needs sharper lanes; four versions died so one franchise could live. A team that has shipped zero reels was pitched five standing fixtures (Wooden Spoon Wednesday, Monday Qualifiers, the fortnightly Fourball, a twice-weekly court, a hole 0 series); the sustainable set is exactly two, and everything whose engine needs an audience that does not exist yet — DM-submitted spooners with scorecard proof, comment-fed dockets, a 19th-hole slide pulled from empty comments, a collab partner "confirmed today" — publishes silence by design, so it died or deferred until the audience is real. Two ideas were strategy decks cosplaying as memes: the courtroom constitution carousel quietly lectured the audience's favourite part of the night, and the "don't open our app on a Tuesday" flex was app-industry inside baseball nobody sends — the restraint belongs in the bio and the product behaviour, not a post. And one kill stands on principle, permanently: Littler's tears appear nowhere, whatever the template heat, because this brand smack-talks the spooner and never real people.
