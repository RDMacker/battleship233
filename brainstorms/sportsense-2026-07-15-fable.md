# Content Think Tank: sportsense — 2026-07-15

The room worked from a reach system that has shifted under coaching content. Private sends and saves have overtaken likes as the signals that decide distribution (sends weighted roughly three to five times likes), raw phone-shot face-to-camera now out-travels polished production as AI floods the feeds, carousels earn a free second impression because Instagram re-serves an ignored post 24 to 48 hours later with slide 2 as the new cover, and a Reel is won or lost on a hook inside the first one to three seconds. Over all of it hangs the argument this brand exists to join: a fully automated MTB coaching app with no human in the loop shipped in May, a 2026 report found 91% of coaches already use AI while insisting it cannot replace human connection, and the position between those two facts, that the real threat to a coach is the unserved gap between sessions rather than replacement, is the ground sportsense can hold.

## The slate

Thirty ideas merged down to twelve. Five personas converged on the same six formats, so most of the cutting was picking winners inside clusters, and every entry below carries its rework conditions already folded in. One rule rides across all of them: the overlay appears as evidence inside somebody's argument, never as the star of its own post.

### Reels and Shorts

**the feedback i actually sent an athlete** (reel, boldness 1 of 5)
Rich holds a phone and narrates one real video-feedback review he delivered that week, opening cold on the athlete's clip with the correction landed inside fifteen seconds, then the drill assigned before the next ride, one take, no cuts. Every genuine review is an episode, so the format restocks itself for as long as the coaching business runs, and it quietly performs the two-way loop that one-way platforms cannot show. Athlete consent is captured on camera, and the narration stays on the correction, never the interface.
Hook: "here is the exact feedback i sent an athlete this morning"
Why now: Mosseri's memo has low-production face-to-camera out-sharing polish for sends, and founder-led unpolished B2B video is beating brand production at 30 to 90 seconds.
Critics flagged: the skeptic called founder-narrates-work the default authenticity play and set the fix now baked in (open on the athlete's clip, never on Rich warming up); the operator scored it 9 as the zero-state start, day-one footage with the least ad-smell on the slate.

**i'll ride your mistake** (reel, boldness 5 of 5)
A real client question appears on screen and instead of explaining, the coach deliberately rides the feature with that exact fault reproduced in his own body, freezes it under the overlay, then rides the fix, so the wrong and the right come from the same rider on the same feature seconds apart. Every question spawns an episode and the pool of common faults never empties, which is why golf pros have run the equivalent for decades. Constrained to low-consequence faults on mellow features, with prompts seeded from real client questions until the comments can supply them.
Hook: "you asked why you nose-dive, so watch me do it on purpose."
Why now: reply-with-video fused with the wrong-vs-right correction, the most reliable save-and-send unit in coaching content, and the deliberate-error move is unclaimed in MTB.
Critics flagged: the audience critic (9) and skeptic (8) called it the strongest pure reel on the slate; the operator's safety constraint is folded in as a hard rule, because a coach injuring himself performing errors could end the channel.

**one myth, one overlay** (Short, cross-posted unchanged as a reel, boldness 3 of 5)
A trusted cue lands in the first second, one overlay frame shows what the cue actually does to the bike, then the corrected position on the same rider, under 30 seconds with the payoff up front. The pilot is "get your weight back", and the rotation is chosen by whichever cue the comments defend hardest, so the audience restocks the format for months. The evidence beat is the same rider hips-back versus hips-low, letting the front-wheel contact argue instead of authority.
Hook: "get your weight back is why your front wheel keeps washing out"
Why now: myth-busting with one specific piece of evidence is a top DM-share short format, and the weight-back fight is public right now among twenty-year coaches.
Critics flagged: three personas wrote this identically (insider-4 kept as the tightest cut, absorbing thoughtleader-4's same-rider comparison and analyst-3's slot); the skeptic warned that myth-busting is the most saturated coaching format alive and the overlay receipt is the only thing keeping it out of the slop pile.

**read the rider** (reel franchise, boldness 4 of 5)
Four seconds of a rider's line, a hard freeze, an on-screen prompt to comment green, blue or black, then the coach's reveal naming one visible tell with a single overlay frame as the receipt, production-identical every episode. The comment section does the reach work and the invited submissions are exactly the clips a remote tier runs on, so the format eventually fuels itself; until then it is seeded from consenting client clips. The "give me four seconds and i'll tell you if you've ever been coached" line survives as an occasional flagship hook, softened away from publicly gatekeeping strangers.
Hook: "you can tell this rider's level in four seconds"
Why now: guess-the-elo and guess-the-rank are proven endlessly repeatable in chess and esports, TikTok shipped official polling for the mechanic, and nobody in MTB coaching runs it.
Critics flagged: arbitration merged maverick-1 into producer-2's spine after a three-way split (the skeptic backed the coached-or-not bet, the audience backed the cleaner game board, the operator flagged the mean edge and the empty submission pipeline); the resolution is one franchise, softened and self-seeded.

**the skeptics are right** (reel, occasional series, boldness 4 of 5)
A raw one-take founder reel opens by agreeing with the audience's suspicion, that personalized coaching is a relationship and not a formula, then turns to the uncomfortable arithmetic in the middle beat, a free app with no coach in it now sees your athlete more often than you do, and closes on the gap as infrastructure the coach should own rather than territory conceded to an app. It repeats whenever a new automation claim makes the rounds, which in 2026 is often. The whole category stays de-named, and the brand's own name stays out of the concession or the trust move collapses into a setup.
Hook: "the skeptics are right, an app is not your coach"
Why now: a fully automated MTB coaching app shipped in May with no human in the loop, while 91% of coaches use AI and insist it cannot replace connection.
Critics flagged: arbitration merged insider-6's concession open with thoughtleader-2's gap argument into one de-named reel; maverick-4's named-app grading died on two kill votes, and its "scored 82, missed the thing that will crash him" beat is allowed back only over a blurred, unnamed mock readout.

**the same rider, before and after one fix** (reel template, boldness 1 of 5, parked)
One athlete rides the same feature before and after a single correction, side by side under the overlay marking the one joint angle that changed, and the template never varies: freeze, cue, result. Every coached athlete eventually generates a pair, so the format compounds with the roster rather than running dry. Parked until the remote pipeline produces genuine pairs, and never staged, because a faked before is the fastest way to lose the exact audience this brand needs.
Hook: "he cased this jump for two years, then his coach changed one angle"
Why now: the before/after reveal is the most proven save-and-send unit in coaching content (the best-known MTB version sits near 20M views) and sends are weighted three to five times likes.
Critics flagged: all three positive on the template; the operator parked it because real coached pairs do not exist at zero state and cannot be manufactured.

**two openings, one fix** (production pipeline, Trial Reels plus the second-zero recut, boldness 2 of 5)
Every mechanism reel is shot once with two different first lines, one problem-led and one myth-led, run as Trial Reels to non-followers, and only the version clearing a sends-per-reach bar touches the grid; the winner is then recut so the payoff line is spoken at second zero and shipped to Shorts unchanged, ending cold with no ask. This is not visible content, it is the discipline every other reel passes through, which is why it never runs out: it is never a topic. Merged from producer-4's hook jury and producer-5's Shorts recut into one production rule.
Hook: opening a, "your athlete keeps casing that jump because of what happens two seconds earlier"; opening b, "stop telling them to pump harder, the jump was lost at the lip"
Why now: Trial Reels return cold-audience metrics with no grid downside, a reel holding over 60% through second three can out-reach a weak one five to ten times, and the Shorts keep-watching decision happens at or before one second.
Critics flagged: all three advanced both halves with the same instruction, run it as pipeline and editing hygiene, never ship it as a concept.

### Carousels

**it was never an objection** (carousel, boldness 3 of 5)
Producer-1's fixed seven-slide spine opens on the athlete's line in their own words, a cropped text message reading "i love training with you, but i can't come more often", with slide 2 held in reserve as the withheld number ("that message is worth about $2,400 a year") so the 24-to-48-hour re-serve gets its own standalone hook. The pilot carries thoughtleader-1's thesis, that the line every sales course teaches coaches to handle was never an objection, it is a request for a product that does not exist yet. The symptom rotates weekly (the cancellation line, the roster wall, the clinic forgetting curve), so the library keeps the spine stocked, and the overlay appears exactly once, as the evidence slide.
Hook: cover, "i love training with you, but i can't come more often"; slide 2 re-serve cover, "it was never the price"
Why now: carousels run roughly 1.92% engagement against 0.50% for Reels, and almost nobody designs for the slide-2 re-serve.
Critics flagged: unanimous cluster winner, with analyst-2 and insider-2 folded in as future editions and thoughtleader-1 donating the pilot thesis; the standing warning is to ration the 50%-at-six-months and 38% lost-motivation stats across editions rather than leaning on them every week.

**the whodunnit frame** (carousel, boldness 4 of 5)
Slide 1 freezes one rider mid-feature under a withheld number, and the middle slides eliminate the audience's pet suspects one at a time (not the brakes, not the tire pressure, not the line) on cropped zooms of the same frame, until the real mechanism lands with the full overlay and joint angles. Detective elimination structurally completes the swipe, and every new clip carries a fresh suspect list, so the format renews with the footage. The first quarter is seeded from Rich's own and client footage, and the cover number must be measured off video timestamps, never invented.
Hook: "somewhere in this frame, 8 km/h went missing."
Why now: it fuses pause-and-guess with the incomplete-cover rules on the highest-engagement feed format, and no MTB account runs elimination.
Critics flagged: all three positive as a standalone; the conditions folded in are the measured number (the skeptic's bar) and self-seeded footage until a submission pipeline exists (the operator's).

### Stories

**the line check loop** (Stories mechanic, boldness 4 of 5, deferred)
Producer-3's three-beat weekly spine holds: Monday a frozen frame under a poll (where did the speed go, entry or exit), Wednesday the cancel-line question sticker inherited from analyst-4, Friday the overlay reveal plus one worked reply, now carrying maverick-3's season scoreboard kept playful (crowd 3, coach 9, never scolding). It is built directly against what Stories rank on, replies, sticker taps and the DMs they open, and the Friday reveals stockpile material for reels and the newsletter, so the loop feeds the rest of the system. Deferred until the Reels and Shorts funnel builds a follower base, with the reply-every-answer commitment capped so it cannot bury a solo founder.
Hook: Monday's opener, a rider frozen mid-corner under "where did the speed go, entry or exit"
Why now: the 2026 Stories update weights replies, sticker taps and resulting DMs, and the poll sticker earns the most taps for the least effort.
Critics flagged: arbitration collapsed five near-identical loops into this one (the scoreboard and the cancel-line sticker absorbed, the injury-retell and adaptive-plan variants dead); the operator's deferral stands because Stories have no cold discovery at zero followers.

### Email and newsletter

**the one-day clinic is your leakiest product** (newsletter, boldness 4 of 5)
The issue tells coaches their flagship product is where the value leaks, argued entirely with what coaches themselves admit in public, that students forget the material by the drive home and lasting skills are never formed in a single day, then repositions the clinic as the acquisition front end of a structured between-session tier. Two workflows follow, turning clinic material into an assigned progression with video check-ins and scheduling the renewal call, closing on one reply ask. The named coaching businesses are de-named and their admissions quoted respectfully as what experienced coaches say about their own trade, because those coaches are the market, not props, and it ships as an edition on the single newsletter spine rather than a second parallel send.
Hook: "your athlete forgot the clinic by the drive home"
Why now: NICA's season opened July 1 with roughly 15,000 volunteer and part-time coaches entering peak clinic season.
Critics flagged: the audience critic called it the strongest newsletter thesis on the slate; the operator's de-naming condition is folded in, along with the reminder that this is the fifth format leaning on the same retention stats, so ration them.

**the $250 fee hiding inside a $5,000 coaching year** (newsletter, boldness 2 of 5)
The issue opens on documented fee math before any product is named, a 5% transaction fee is $250 on $5,000 and advertised prices run 40 to 60% higher once payments and add-ons stack, then delivers two workflows, pricing a remote tier against the published $150 to $400 monthly benchmark and scheduling the goal-review call four to six weeks before a package ends. Every vendor stays unnamed, and the same issue discloses sportsense's own pricing math so the fee argument cuts both ways instead of reading as a rival's sniping. It repeats because there is a different hidden-cost or retention number every edition, and it is the owned-layer spine to start building now.
Hook: subject line, "the $250 fee hiding inside a $5,000 coaching year"
Why now: creator newsletters run 35 to 50% opens against 22 to 32% general, and the fee anger is live and quantified.
Critics flagged: cluster keeper over insider-5, whose named vendor quote had to go anyway; the audience critic's condition, disclose your own pricing in the same issue or trust dies on open two, is now part of the format.

Two owned-layer mechanics stay on the bench with conditions attached: maverick-6's friday frame league (diagnose the frame before you scroll, with a named reader leaderboard) waits until replies clear roughly twenty per issue and seeds its entrants from the Stories game, and producer-6's polls newsletter (the week's sticker data quoted back to coaches) waits until the Stories loop generates real volume, printing raw counts like 31 of 51 coaches rather than percentages while the sample is small.

## Wildcards worth the risk

**four grand on the bike, zero on the rider** (reel). The rider-versus-bike argument splits every comment section close to 50/50 and "send this to the rider who just ordered another wheelset" is a real send with a timely peg, the €100,000 belt-drive bounty still unclaimed while the winning margin at La Thuile was 0.961 seconds of technique no drivetrain closes. The risk is that this is the most recycled sermon in MTB content and the skeptic voted to kill it as costume, so it runs only with the rights fix (own footage or a graphic, never a World Cup broadcast frame) and an argument sharpened past the sermon everyone has already heard.

**i'll ride your mistake**. Already on the slate, counted here because its risk is physical rather than editorial: a coach reproducing faults on camera is an injury risk that could end the channel in one bad take. The safety constraint, low-consequence faults on mellow features from a pre-agreed fault list, is not a nicety, it is the entire bet.

**the whodunnit frame's measured-number bar**. The format's credibility rests on the withheld cover number being a real delta measured off video timestamps. The risk is drift toward invented precision when a clip resists measurement, and one faked number collapses the detective posture the whole franchise trades on.

**the friday frame league**. Converting the reply ask into a diagnosis game with a named leaderboard is the strongest email mechanic in the room, and the risk is timing: launch before the list clears about twenty replies per issue and the league publishes silence from issue one, so it waits and seeds from the Stories scoreboard.

## Killed, and why

Most of the killing was convergence rather than weakness. Five personas independently wrote the same six formats (the diagnostic guessing game, the gap carousel, the weight-back myth debunk, the weekly Stories loop, the fee newsletter, the AI-position piece), so roughly half the slate died as duplicates of its own winners, folded in as episodes, hooks or evidence beats. The rest died on three patterns. Competitor naming and injury attribution went first: the named-app grading reel, the review-reading reel and the Garmin injury Story were killed or de-named, because a vendor sniping rivals is exactly what a skeptical coach expects from software companies, and the defamation exposure on attributing an injury to a named brand off a forum thread was real. Anything with overlay-as-star ad smell was cut or reworked, since to revenue-motivated coaches a software showcase reads as an ad no matter how good the riding is. And zero-state dependencies did the quiet damage: formats that needed submissions, Stories reach or an email list that do not exist yet (the crowd-vs-coach scoreboard, the reader leaderboard, the polls newsletter, the bare cancel-line poll) were deferred or absorbed into stronger spines rather than shipped to an empty room.

## The build order

1. The feedback-narration reel first, because the footage is feedback Rich already sends as part of coaching, one phone take, the least ad-smell on the slate.
2. The myth Short second, an afternoon of self-filmed wrong-versus-right descent clips, cross-posted as a Reel unchanged.
3. The gap carousel third, coverable with one client clip plus public stats on the fixed seven-slide spine.
4. The whodunnit fourth, seeded from Rich's own and client footage with a genuinely measured cover number.
5. Trial Reels and the second-zero recut layered across every reel from week one, the hook jury on the way in and the free Shorts surface on the way out.
6. The newsletter spine started now as the owned layer, fee math first, the clinic-leaks thesis in the rotation while clinic season runs.
7. The Stories loop and the data-led newsletter stay deferred until the Reels build a follower base worth polling.
8. The before/after template stays parked until the pipeline produces real coached pairs, and it is never staged.
9. i'll ride your mistake enters once prompts from real client questions exist and the safe fault list is written.

The one risk that outlives this session is positioning, not production: the overlay must stay evidence inside each format and never become the star, or the whole account reads as a software ad to the skeptical, revenue-motivated coaches it is for. Every format above leads with the coach's problem, the rider, or the myth, and the overlay shows up exactly where a receipt would.
