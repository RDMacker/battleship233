# Pub Golf Pro — Creative & Brand Brief

Brief this file to any agent (human or AI) producing marketing creative —
social posts, ads, email, merch, video scripts. It captures brand standards
(fixed) and creative strategy (the working thesis). The product's own
reasoning lives in `pub-golf-pro/docs/STRATEGY.md` — read that for *why* the
app behaves the way it does; this doc is about how to *talk about it*.

---

## What it is, in one breath

Pub Golf Pro turns a pub crawl into a round of golf. Each bar is a hole, each
drink has a par (sips to finish), players log strokes and penalties, and the
night ends in a shareable scorecard. Free web app, no download.

Say it like this, not like a feature list: **"You love to drink. You love a
pub crawl. You love to compete."** The product is the third sentence — most
crawls have no structure and no proof anything happened. Pub Golf Pro adds
the score.

## Brand standards (fixed — don't deviate without a reason)

### Name & handles
- Product name: **Pub Golf Pro** (title case in prose; the wordmark in
  footers/logos is set in ALL CAPS with wide letter-spacing).
- Instagram / social handle: **@pubgolf.pro**
- URL: **pubgolf.pro**

### Color palette
Pulled directly from the live app (`src/index.css`), not invented for
marketing — the ad creative and the product must feel like the same brand.

| Role | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Fairway green | `#0C3B23` | Primary background / primary text-on-gold |
| Gold | `#F4B625` | Primary accent, CTAs, secondary background |
| Cream | `#F8F1DF` | Body text on green, soft off-white |
| Muted green (card fill) | `#11492B` | Cards/rows on a green background |
| Loss red | `#7A2E2E` | Used sparingly — last place, penalties, "out of bounds" |

Only two backgrounds exist: **green** or **gold**. Every other color is text
or an accent on top of one of those two. Don't introduce a third background
color for marketing assets.

### Typography
- **Outfit** (weight 800–900) — all display headlines and big numbers.
- **Space Grotesk** (weight 400–700) — supporting copy, kickers, footers,
  handles.
- Sentence case throughout, including headlines. No ALL CAPS body copy — the
  only all-caps text is the small kicker line and the wordmark lockup.

### Visual motifs
- **The flag pin** (a simple flagstick + pennant, no ball) is the one brand
  mark. Use it small, in the footer lockup, and optionally once bigger as a
  hero visual. Don't add a golf ball, tee, or club — the flag alone reads as
  "golf" without looking like a golf-brand rip-off.
- **Dotted fairway line** — a soft dashed arc in the background, low
  opacity (~15–20%), suggesting a ball's flight path. Decorative only, never
  competes with text.
- Every asset carries a thin rounded-corner border inset from the edge and a
  bottom lockup: flag + "PUB GOLF PRO" wordmark bottom-left, "@pubgolf.pro"
  bottom-right, separated by a hairline.
- Flat design. No gradients, no drop shadows, no photos of real bars/drinks
  in owned creative — the brand is graphic/typographic, not photographic.
  (UGC and recap screenshots from real users, and meme posts that caption
  found movie or reaction stills, are the exceptions — see Content Pillars.)

### Voice
Confident, funny, a little competitive-smack-talk — a mate egging you on,
not a corporate brand account.

- Short declarative sentences. Full stops, not exclamation marks.
- Talk to the group, not the individual — "your crew," "the crawl," "the
  round," not "you" in isolation.
- Golf vocabulary is the running joke: holes, par, strokes, hazards,
  bunkers, birdies, the green jacket, the wooden spoon. Use it correctly and
  consistently so regulars start using the terms themselves.
- Never moralize about drinking. Competence and humor, not a warning label.
  (The one earnest exception: casual/non-alcoholic players are framed as
  fully in the game, not as an asterisk — see Positioning.)
- Avoid generic party-brand clichés: "epic," "lit," "unforgettable" — cut on
  sight unless doing real work in a specific sentence.

## Positioning

**This is an event app, not an engagement app.** Nobody opens Pub Golf Pro
on a random Tuesday. The unit of growth is the crawl, not the daily user —
so creative should not chase habitual use. Every asset should either:

1. Get a host to start a crawl (top of funnel), or
2. Make a recap/scorecard worth posting or sending to a group chat
   (the actual growth loop — see below).

**The recap is the marketing.** Per `STRATEGY.md`, the post-round scorecard
is the one screen designed to leave the app and travel into a group chat
with no app around it. Every real crawl produces a piece of content the
product itself generates — this is more valuable long-term than any static
ad, and creative direction should treat user-generated recaps as the hero
content, with brand-made posts as the top-of-funnel bridge to it.

**Awards, not just rankings.** A leaderboard gives the winner a reason to
post. Awards give everyone a reason to post — the "Wooden Spoon" (last
place) is often the funniest, most-shared result of the night. Marketing
copy should reflect this: losing is content, not something to be quiet
about.

**Competitive mode is the wedge; casual is the on-ramp.** Marketing should
foreground the competitive, golf-scored version — that's the differentiated
and funny thing. Casual crawls exist and should be mentioned (a small
"casual crawls also supported" footnote is enough), not led with.

## Audience & occasions

Primary: groups aged low-20s to mid-30s organizing a big social night —
usually one motivated host planning for 4–18 people. High-intent occasions,
in rough priority order:

1. **Stag dos / hen dos** — highest spend, highest intent, most likely to
   actually use scoring and penalties seriously.
2. **Birthdays**
3. **Daytime sessions** (Christmas markets, festival days, "day drinking
   with structure")
4. **Work socials / parties** generally

Geography-agnostic — the product travels to any city with more than a
couple of pubs walking distance apart. Don't hard-code a specific city in
brand assets; use generic occasion framing instead ("your city," "the
crawl") unless a specific campaign targets one market.

## Content pillars (what to make, and why)

These map to the post types already built and tested (see
`instagram-assets/` for the current 9-slide set). Use this as the menu when
briefing new creative — every asset should clearly be one of these, not a
grab-bag of "content."

The highest-volume pillar in practice is **meme humor**, which most of the
Instagram and Meta feed now runs on: male-camaraderie movie moments and
reaction images with a meme caption laid over the top (think Jordan
Belfort's "I'm not leaving" from Wolf of Wall Street, or the "you son of a
bitch, I'm in" Schwarzenegger reaction), riffing on drinking, the crew, the
competition, and the wooden spoon. Stag dos are one occasion among several,
not the default frame. Memes are the exception to the flat, graphic-only
rule (they caption found movie and reaction stills); the nine numbered
pillars below are the brand-made graphic posts that support the humor, and
they stay flat and typographic.

1. **Hero / brand statement** — one strong line, minimal, sets the tone.
   ("An epic session. And the scorecard to prove it.")
2. **How to play / rules** — the single most save-and-share-worthy post
   type for a new-to-the-concept audience. Screenshottable, three rules
   max.
3. **Product walkthrough** — plan → share link → score. Bridges to a
   traffic/conversion objective; this is the one to run as a "learn more"
   ad.
4. **Leaderboard / crown a champion** — social proof + stakes. Shows the
   product doing its actual job (a live, real-time scorecard).
5. **Hazards / rules-as-humor** — the funniest, highest organic-reach post
   type. Water hazard = spilled pint, bunker = wrong drink ordered, out of
   bounds = tapping out early. This is the "people tag their mates" post.
6. **Occasion / "made for a proper night"** — broad, aspirational, used for
   interest-based ad targeting (stag do, hen do, birthday, daytime
   session).
7. **Slogan / attitude** — pure brand voice, minimal product explanation.
   Works as a thumbnail and a quote-card.
8. **Feature spotlight** — currently the AI crawl planner ("let the app
   plan it," marked beta). Use for "can't be bothered organizing"
   audiences who'd never build a crawl manually.
9. **Direct CTA** — the closer. Domain in a gold pill, one-line value prop
   underneath. Pair with a traffic/conversion ad objective.

A tenth pillar to build toward once real usage exists: **UGC recap
reposts** — actual scorecards and award screenshots from real crawls,
reposted with permission. This is the highest-trust content type available
and should eventually anchor the feed once volume exists; static
brand-made posts are the bridge until then.

## What to avoid

- Don't invent new taglines that contradict the ones already in rotation
  (see Approved Lines below) without a clear reason — consistency compounds
  on a new account.
- Don't lead with the AI planner or any single feature as the hero message
  — it's a supporting pillar, not the pitch.
- Don't use real bar/venue names or logos in owned creative without
  clearance.
- Don't imply the app tracks or shares users' drinking data outside their
  own crawl — no "leaderboard vs. strangers," no cross-crawl comparison
  language. Each crawl is its own self-contained round.
- Don't design around daily-engagement mechanics (streaks, daily
  challenges, notifications nagging you to open the app) — this is
  explicitly not that kind of product; see Positioning above.
- Don't moralize about alcohol. Handle the non-drinker/designated-driver
  case with the same competitive respect as everyone else (they play at
  "par 1"), never as a caveat or a warning.

## Approved lines (current rotation)

Reuse or riff on these rather than starting from zero — they're tuned to
the brand voice and have already been through design review:

- "You love to drink. You love a pub crawl. You love to compete."
- "An epic session. And the scorecard to prove it."
- "Make the night official with a pub golf scorecard."
- "How to play: holes = pubs to stop at, strokes = sips per drink, lowest
  score wins. (Casual crawls also supported.)"
- "Crown a champion."
- "Know the hazards." (water hazard / bunker / out of bounds)
- "Made for a proper night."
- "Let the app plan it." (AI planner, beta)
- "Your round starts now." / "Free to play. Grab the crew. Tee off."

## Quick brief template

When commissioning a new asset, specify:

1. **Pillar** (which of the 9 above)
2. **Background** — green or gold (pick for contrast/rhythm against
   neighboring posts, not arbitrarily)
3. **One line of copy** — the whole point of the post, in one sentence
4. **Format** — 1:1 square (feed/ads default) or 9:16 (Stories/Reels)
5. **Objective** — awareness (humor/occasion pillars) vs. traffic/CTA
   (walkthrough, direct CTA pillars)

Keep every asset to one idea. If a brief has two ideas, it's two posts.
